Magnificient Mustang

Mustang, former Buddhist Kingdom stretching from the Annapurna mountain range, one of the exotic corners of Nepal. With its stunning landscapes and timeless Himalayan villages, Mustang is the stuff of trekking dreams. Mustang feels quite different compared to the rest of the Himalayas of Nepal. Upper Mustang was a restricted demilitarized area until 1992, which makes it one of the most preserved regions in the world due to its relative isolation from the outside world, with a majority of the population still speaking traditional Tibetic languages. It is one of the popular areas for trekking and can be visited year around regardless of season.  

The best season to visit Mustang is from the beginning of March to late December. January and February months are also suitable to Visit Lower Mustang. The best season for Upper Mustang is from March to November. 

Mustang is home to one of the greatest concentrations of human carved cliff caves. Archaeologists and researchers have explored these caves and found partially mummified human bodies and skeletons which are at least 2,000-3,000 years old. No one has known who built these huge caves and why they were built. Explorers found stupas, decorative art and paintings depicting various forms of the Buddha, often with disciples, supplicants and attendants, with some mural paintings showing sub-tropical themes containing palm trees, billowing Indian textiles and birds.

Another place to visit in Mustang is Muktinath temple. It is located in Muktinath Valley at the foot of Thorong La which is a Vishnu Temple sacred to both Hindus and Buddhists. It is also one of the world’s highest temples (altitude 3,800m).
